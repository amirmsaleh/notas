\begin{resumo}[English]
Originally, a glyph referred to any carved or painted sign, such as the glyphs of the Mayan script and Egyptian hieroglyphs. Interest in glyphs grew in Europe during the mid-19th century, following the publication of books by John Lloyd Stephens and Frederick Catherwood regarding the Mayan civilization and the indecipherable signs of its script. The books were bestsellers at the time, and Catherwood's drawings of the glyphs are extremely accurate.
